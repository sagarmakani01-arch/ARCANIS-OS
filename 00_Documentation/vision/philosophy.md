## Core Belief

Computing is ready for its next metamorphosis. The operating system of the future will not be a platform that *runs* AI — it will be a platform that *is* AI.

## The Three Pillars

### 1. AI as Substrate, Not Application

Traditional systems add AI as a layer on top of a legacy kernel. Arcanis weaves intelligence into every level of the stack — from the scheduler and memory manager to the file system and device drivers. AI is not an app you launch; it is the environment you inhabit.

### 2. Declarative Over Imperative

Users and developers should describe *what* they want, not *how* to achieve it. The system infers intent, plans execution, and adapts to changing conditions. Commands become conversations; scripts become policies.

### 3. Symbiotic Agency

The system acts as a partner, not a tool. It learns from behavior, anticipates needs, and operates autonomously within bounds defined by the user. Trust is earned through transparency, explainability, and user control.

## Guiding Principles

- **Radical simplicity** — Complexity is pushed into the system, not onto the user
- **Privacy by default** — All intelligence is local unless explicitly shared
- **Open foundation** — Core research and protocols are open; commercial products build on top
- **Deterministic when needed** — AI augments but never replaces predictability for critical paths
- **Continuous evolution** — The system learns and improves without manual updates
